The rise of chatbots, voice assistants, and LLMs made talking to machines feel... human. Conversations became interfaces. From clippy to ChatGPT, we learned to ask, not just click.

Hello, how do I reset my password?
Have you tried turning it off and on again?
"When conversations became commands, interfaces disappeared."

We crossed the interface. Now it senses, predicts, and adapts. The line between user and system has blurred. You don't use it. You are it.

Are we still waiting for this future—or already living it?
